---
title: "Asking customers to leave a review"
description: "An overview of all the ways you can ask customers to leave a review in Magnora."
---

Magnora gives you several ways to ask customers for reviews. Here's a quick overview:

| Method | How it's used |
|---|---|
| Automated post-purchase review requests | The primary way to collect reviews. Flows automatically send a request after every purchase. |
| Manual review link | A standalone link customers can use to review any product or update a previous review. |
| On-site write a review button | Part of the product page widget — lets customers leave a review directly on your storefront. |
| One-time campaign | Send a one-time review request to past customers who were never asked. |
| Post-submission multi-product prompt | After submitting a review, customers are prompted to review other items from the same order. |

## Automated post-purchase review requests

Post-purchase review requests are automatically sent to customers after they place an order. Over 90% of all reviews come through this method — it's the most important flow to get right.

Go to [Flows](https://app.magnora.io/flows) in your Magnora admin to set this up. You can send requests directly from Magnora's email system, or push events to an integration like Klaviyo or Postscript to send from your own email/SMS platform.

**Things to know:**
- Requests are personalised to what the customer bought and link straight into a verified review form — no extra identity confirmation needed
- If a customer has already reviewed an item, they won't receive another request for it
- Customers submitting via post-purchase links are pre-verified and don't need to confirm their identity again

## Manual review link

A manual review link lets customers confirm their email and then review any product they've previously purchased, edit past reviews, or leave an overall store review.

Access your review link from [Forms](https://app.magnora.io/forms) in your Magnora admin. This is best used as a complement to your automated flows — not a replacement. It works well in support conversations, packaging inserts, or anywhere you want to personally direct a happy customer.

<Note>
  Unlike post-purchase review links, manual review links require customers to confirm their identity — this is an extra step compared to the pre-verified post-purchase experience.
</Note>

## On-site write a review button

This button is automatically included in Magnora's product page widget. It allows customers to leave a review directly from your storefront.

Customers on product pages are primarily there to *read* reviews rather than write them, so don't expect high volumes from this method — but it's a useful fallback. You can disable the button from your [on-site settings](https://app.magnora.io/on_site) if you prefer.

**Things to know:**
- Customers must enter their email and confirm their identity before submitting — this protects against spam and fraudulent reviews
- Unconfirmed reviews are not submitted

## One-time campaigns

Campaigns let you send a review request to past customers who weren't included in your automated flows — for example, customers who ordered before you installed Magnora, or during a gap in your setup.

[Learn how to send a campaign →](/getting-started/send-a-campaign)

## Post-submission multi-product prompt

After a customer submits a review, Magnora automatically prompts them to review any other products from the same order. If they've reviewed everything, they're shown a prompt to leave an overall store review.

This is effective because you already have the customer's attention and your products are front of mind. The convenience of not having to navigate elsewhere also increases conversion.

<Note>
  If you're not displaying store reviews on your site, you can turn off the store review prompt under Forms → [Preferences](https://app.magnora.io/forms/preferences).
</Note>
